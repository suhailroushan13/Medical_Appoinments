A recurring problem in the area of public health is the high rate of patients who do not attend scheduled medical examinations and consultations. In addition to not attending diagnostic support tests, absenteeism reaches a global prevalence of around 25% in specialized outpatient clinics [1]. The investigation of the main reasons for absenteeism in medical appointments is a fundamental factor for continued health care, for improving access to the public health system, for controlling waiting lines and improving the quality of life of the population. Absenteeism from previously scheduled appointments compromises the effectiveness of medical care and creates a series of problems in public health systems around the world. Studies have identified long waiting times for health care as one of the main challenges facing the system [2], [3] [4]. A public opinion poll in Canada [4] showed that 75% of respondents identified reducing the waiting list as a high priority action.
